Top Banner (Yes/No Bars)

Two stacked bars across the top of the dashboard — Yes (blue) and No (red). Each bar has two portions:

Solid blue/red ← counted votes so far
Faint extension ← projected D/R outstanding ballots

How the projection works

The faint portion projects remaining absentee ballots onto Yes/No by party:

  • Dem outstanding → assumed to vote Yes
  • Rep outstanding → assumed to vote No
This is an estimate, not a call. It assumes counted ballots have the same party composition as returned ballots, and that Democrats vote Yes / Republicans vote No on this amendment. Useful visual tool but not definitive.

Reading the percentages

The YES % / NO % text labels show actual counted share only (Yes of Yes+No counted). They do NOT reflect the projection. The bar tells the projection story; the number tells the current standing.

Absentee Status Chips (Ab Status column)

Each of the 133 localities gets a status chip indicating where they are in posting absentee. Derived from ELECT's reportingStatuses feed for each locality's Early Voting and Mailed Absentee methods.

Not Reported Default. Neither EV nor MA posted yet. All 133 start here at 7 PM.
In-Person Only EV posted, MA still pending. Common first transition (EV is machine-tabulated, posts fast).
Mailed Only MA posted first, EV still pending. Less common but possible (e.g., Fairfax bulk XML import).
All Reported Both EV and MA posted (election-night complete). Absentee pool fully reflected in results.
Final Both fully reported (canvassed). Typically reached in the days after — not tonight.
Sort by the Ab Status column to see who's posted and who hasn't. Sorts in the progression above: Not Reported first, Final last. Click again to reverse.

Key Numbers & Concepts

margin_vs_outstanding (summary.json)

Hidden field but the math is visible in the outstanding card. Formula: |Yes - No| - Outstanding.

  • Positive = Outstanding can't mathematically flip the result. Race is called.
  • Negative = Outstanding exceeds the current margin. Race still in play.

Virtual precincts

Some localities use "virtual" precincts (e.g., "CAP" = Central Absentee Precinct) as catch-all buckets on election night. These hold EV/MA totals that get redistributed to real precincts during canvass. Flagged in the ENR feed with isVirtual: true.

Why the absentee data froze at 7 AM

The absentee pipeline (counties.json) runs once per morning from the VAN → DAL join. ENR updates continuously. So "Abs. Returned" stays flat all day; "Abs. Outstanding" drops as ENR posts EV/MA counts.

Tonight's Operator Tips

What to watch for

  • Fairfax County — bulk XML import, typically posts all absentee at once around 7:30-8:00 PM. Big swing in numbers when it lands.
  • First reporting locality — click on it, look at its Yes % vs DemPerf (Yes vs Base column). Early indicator of direction.
  • Stuck "Not Reported" after 9 PM — flag for the ELECT team; may indicate locality reporting issues.
  • Close race check — compare |Yes - No| to Abs. Outstanding. If outstanding exceeds margin, race is still in play regardless of % counted.

If the dashboard looks wrong

  • Hard refresh the browser (Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R)
  • Check the top-right "Results:" timestamp — if it's more than 5 min old, the pipeline may have hung
  • Check metadata.json in the repo — last_updated field
Remember: The "margin_vs_outstanding" math assumes outstanding ballots split the same way as currently-counted ballots. If EV breaks heavily one direction and MA breaks the opposite, early projections can mislead. Wait for several rounds of updates before making calls.
